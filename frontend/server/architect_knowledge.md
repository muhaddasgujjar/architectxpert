---
Pakistan building codes (overview): Provincial building authorities (e.g. LDA in Lahore, CDA in Islamabad, KMC/KDA in Karachi) govern setbacks, height limits, and coverage (ground coverage vs total floor area). Always verify current bylaws for your zone before final design; FAR (floor area ratio) and front/side/rear setbacks change by plot category and road width.
---
Structural grey structure: Typical urban houses use RCC frame with brick/block infill. Damp-proof course (DPC) at plinth, proper roof insulation in hot climates, and lap splicing per structural drawings. Seismic detailing matters in northern regions; follow engineer-of-record for column/beam sizes and stirrup spacing.
---
Energy and comfort: Cross-ventilation, courtyard or stack ventilation, roof insulation (EPS/XPS under screed), and reflective roof finishes reduce cooling load. South/west glazing benefits from deep overhangs or external shading devices. Double-glazed units help noise and AC load in urban corridors.
---
Sustainable materials: Prefer locally sourced aggregate and bricks where quality is certified. RMC from reputable batching plants improves consistency. Low-VOC paints and water-based finishes improve indoor air quality. Rainwater harvesting and greywater reuse may be mandated or incentivized in some housing schemes.
---
Cost planning: Distinguish grey structure, finishes, MEP, and fixtures. Contingency (often 8–15%) covers rate volatility and design changes. Phased procurement of steel and cement can track seasonal pricing; keep payment milestones aligned with site progress and inspections.
